Welcome to the RSVP Pocket Reader


What this is

You are reading right now using RSVP, which stands for Rapid Serial Visual
Presentation. Instead of moving your eyes across a line of text, the reader shows
you one word at a time, in the same fixed place, at a speed you choose. Your eyes
stay still and the words come to you. One letter of each word is tinted and held
on the same spot, so your gaze has a steady anchor and never has to hunt.

Because your eyes are not jumping from word to word, many people find they can
read faster, and with less effort, once the pace starts to feel natural. Begin
slowly, around two hundred words a minute, and nudge it up a little at a time.


What the device does

This is a small, quiet, single-purpose reader. It does one thing, focused
reading of your own books, and otherwise stays out of the way. It works fully
offline. There are no accounts, no network, no advertisements, and nothing
keeping score. Your books live on the device, and it quietly remembers how far
you have read in each one, so you can always pick up where you left off.

On the finished hardware it will be a pocket sized screen with three buttons
along the top and a touchscreen for everything else. What you are using now is
the desktop version of that same reader, so you can try the whole experience
before the hardware exists.


How to use this desktop version

Press the space bar to start reading, and press it again to pause. The word stays
on the screen while you are paused, asking nothing of you, until you are ready to
continue.

To change the speed, press the up and down arrow keys, or the comma and period
keys. On the real device these are the three top buttons: slower, play and pause,
and faster.

Click on the word in the middle of the screen to open the menu. The mouse stands
in for the touchscreen, so a click is a tap. In the menu you will find the
Library, where you choose a book, add a new one, or remove one you have finished;
Chapters, for jumping around within a book; Settings, for speed, font, brightness,
low power mode, the automatic off timer, and more; Stats, to see your progress and
how long you have read; and About. Tap an item to choose it, and swipe right with
the mouse, or press the Escape key, to step back.

If you lose your place, or you want to reread something you missed, press the Tab
key. The whole book appears as ordinary text with your current word highlighted.
Click any word there to send the reader to that spot, then press Tab again to
return to reading.

When you are done, press the letter q, or the Escape key, to quit. Whatever you
were reading will be waiting exactly where you left it the next time you open the
reader.

That is everything. Choose a comfortable speed, press the space bar, and let the
words come to you, one at a time, all the way to the end.
